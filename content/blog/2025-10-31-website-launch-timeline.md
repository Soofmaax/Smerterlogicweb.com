# How long to get your website live? (The real timeline — no BS)

<p>You’ve decided to build your website. First question: <strong>how long until it’s live?</strong></p>
<p>A freelancer says “3 weeks”. An agency says “3 months”. Your brother‑in‑law says he did it in a weekend on Wix. Who’s right?</p>
<p>Since 2020 I’ve built websites for SMBs. The truth: <strong>it depends</strong> — mostly on <strong>you</strong> (speed of feedback and content) and on complexity.</p>
<p>Here are the <strong>5 phases</strong>, the <strong>realistic timelines</strong> and <strong>how to speed up without sacrificing quality</strong>.</p>

<h2>Why timelines vary (the big variables)</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Technical complexity</strong>: simple brochure (5 pages) vs e‑commerce/app (accounts, payments, CRM).</li>
  <li><strong>Page count</strong>: 5 pages ≠ 20 pages (design, copy, integration, testing).</li>
  <li><strong>Design approach</strong>: bespoke (2–8 weeks) vs adapted template (~1 week).</li>
  <li><strong>Advanced features</strong>: booking, payments, ERP/CRM add 1–4 weeks each.</li>
  <li><strong>Your responsiveness</strong>: slow approvals and late content add weeks.</li>
</ul>

<h2>The 5 project phases</h2>
<h3>1) Strategy & brief</h3>
<p>Goals, audience, sitemap, scope (pages/features/content).</p>
<p><strong>Timeline</strong>: 1–4 weeks. Key factor: a clear brief.</p>

<h3>2) UX/UI design</h3>
<p>Architecture, mockups, prototypes. <strong>Bespoke</strong>: 2–8 weeks. <strong>Template</strong>: ~1 week.</p>
<p><strong>Risk</strong>: long feedback loops.</p>

<h3>3) Development & integration</h3>
<p>Implement mockups, CMS if needed, features (forms, payments, client area…).</p>
<p><strong>Timeline</strong>: 4–24 weeks depending on scope. Avoid scope creep.</p>

<h3>4) Content, review & QA</h3>
<p>Integrate copy/photos/video, cross‑browser/device QA and fixes.</p>
<p><strong>Timeline</strong>: 2–6 weeks. <strong>Bottleneck</strong>: client content delivery.</p>

<h3>5) Go‑live & follow‑up</h3>
<p>Deployment, DNS, SSL, Analytics/Search Console, final checks.</p>
<p><strong>Timeline</strong>: 1–3 weeks. Short hypercare period recommended.</p>

<h2>Why projects take 8 months instead of 2</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>No content</strong>: +4–12 weeks lost.</li>
  <li><strong>Slow approvals</strong>: >3 days per cycle adds 1–2 weeks.</li>
  <li><strong>Scope creep</strong>: changing requirements mid‑project.</li>
</ul>

<h2>How to accelerate (without cutting corners)</h2>
<ol>
  <li><strong>Deliver all content upfront</strong>: final copy, pro photos, brand assets.</li>
  <li><strong>Approve in 48–72h</strong> each step: every day saved prevents 2 days of project delay.</li>
  <li><strong>Limit iterations</strong>: 1–2 cycles max, respect the agreed scope.</li>
</ol>
<p>No time to write? Outsource copywriting and save 2–4 weeks.</p>

<h2>Don’t confuse speed with haste</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Skipping UX → confusing navigation, lower conversion.</li>
  <li>Skipping SEO/perf → poor visibility and slower pages.</li>
  <li>Rushed code → technical debt and costly evolutions later.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Verdict</h2>
<p>A professional brochure site <strong>can</strong> be delivered in <strong>7–13 weeks</strong> with prepared content, fast approvals and a stable scope; otherwise expect <strong>14–34 weeks</strong>.</p>

<h2>FAQ</h2>
<h3>How long for a brochure website?</h3>
<p>7–13 weeks if you’re responsive. 14–34 weeks if approvals/content drag. Short timelines require a clear brief, content in week 1, 48–72h approvals and a stable scope.</p>
<h3>What are the 5 phases?</h3>
<p>1) Strategy/brief; 2) Design; 3) Dev; 4) Content/QA; 5) Go‑live/follow‑up. Bottleneck: content.</p>
<h3>Why do some say 3 weeks and others 3 months?</h3>
<p>3 weeks = very simple site (5 pages, template, content ready, no advanced features). 3 months = professional site (10 pages, bespoke design, SEO, integrations). Beware unrealistic promises.</p>
<h3>Can we speed up without harming quality?</h3>
<p>Yes: remove waiting (content ready, fast approvals), not vital steps (UX, QA, SEO). Outsource copy if needed.</p>
<h3>What causes the biggest delays?</h3>
<p>Missing content, slow approvals, scope changes. Prepare before signing and stick to the scope.</p>
<h3>Is a 1‑week website realistic?</h3>
<p>Possible with 3–5 pages, adapted template, content ready, and zero advanced features. Otherwise expect 3–4 weeks minimum.</p>
