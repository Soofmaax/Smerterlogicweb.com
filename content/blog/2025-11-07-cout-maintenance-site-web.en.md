# Technical maintenance: who handles it and at what cost?

<p>Your site has been live for six months. It works; it brings clients. Then one morning: <strong>error</strong>, blank page, or “This site has been hacked”. Urgency, invoice, lost clients. <strong>How to avoid this?</strong> With adapted technical maintenance — and a controlled budget.</p>
<p>Since 2020 I’ve built and maintained SMB sites. <strong>A site without maintenance is a ticking time‑bomb</strong>: bugs, slowdowns, vulnerabilities, hacks. <em>Average outage cost</em>: €1,000–€50,000. <em>Preventive cost</em>: €30–€500/month depending on provider.</p>
<p>In this article: who handles it (you, freelancer, agency), the <strong>real annual costs</strong>, and the <strong>cost of no‑maintenance</strong>. By the end, you’ll know how much to invest to <strong>sleep peacefully</strong>.</p>

<h2>The myth of “built once, works forever”</h2>
<p>A website isn’t a piece of furniture. It’s a living system, dependent on evolving technologies.</p>

<h3>Three pillars that require continuous maintenance</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Performance</strong>: WordPress <em>slows down</em> (cluttered DB, unoptimized images, plugins). Without upkeep → 2s → 5s, SEO drops, bounce rises.</li>
  <li><strong>Security</strong>: 43% of WordPress compromises come from outdated plugins/themes. Patches ship weekly. <strong>Without updates</strong> → vulnerable.</li>
  <li><strong>Compatibility</strong>: browsers evolve (Chrome/Safari/Firefox). A Safari change can break an iPhone form. <strong>Regular testing</strong> is essential.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Non‑maintenance fixed costs (baseline)</h2>
<h3>Domain: €10–€50/year</h3>
<p>Enable auto‑renewal and an alert 1 month before expiry.</p>
<h3>Hosting: €50–€500/year</h3>
<p>WordPress showcase: shared €50–€120/year. A €600/year VPS for 5 pages? <strong>Unnecessary</strong>. <em>Static</em>: Netlify/Vercel <strong>free</strong>.</p>
<h3>Plugin/theme licenses (WordPress): €50–€400/year</h3>
<p>E.g., WP Rocket, Elementor Pro, Yoast SEO, Wordfence. Ask for the <strong>list & annual cost</strong> <em>before</em> delivery.</p>

<h2>Three maintenance options: DIY, freelancer, agency</h2>

<h3>Option 1 — DIY (yourself)</h3>
<p><strong>Direct cost</strong>: €0/month. <strong>Time</strong>: 2–4h/month. <strong>Risk</strong>: high (errors, missed backups, plugin conflicts). One emergency: €80/h × 3h = €240 + lost leads.</p>
<p><em>Acceptable</em> only if you’re a web developer with time.</p>

<h3>Option 2 — Freelancer (€50–€100/month)</h3>
<p>Core/plugins/theme updates, backups, security, minor fixes, email responses.</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Advantages</strong>: competitive pricing, targeted expertise, direct relationship.</li>
  <li><strong>Limit</strong>: unavailability (vacation/illness). Ask for <strong>backup</strong> (peer network).</li>
</ul>

<h3>Option 3 — Agency (€150–€500/month)</h3>
<p>Team, process, <strong>SLA</strong> (guaranteed response/resolution times), level‑3 support (engineer) during crises.</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Advantages</strong>: continuity, reporting, guaranteed timelines.</li>
  <li><strong>Cost</strong>: higher, but <em>insurance</em> against costly outages.</li>
</ul>
<p>Tip: premium licenses often <strong>pooled</strong> → savings (€200–€400/year).</p>

<h2>The real cost of no‑maintenance</h2>
<h3>Direct cost</h3>
<p>4‑hour outage: €1,000–€5,000 (revenue + leads + credibility). Major outage (hack, rebuild): €10,000–€50,000.</p>
<p>Over 2 years: high probability without maintenance. <strong>Expected cost</strong> ≈ €8,000 vs <strong>maintenance</strong> €1,200 → <strong>€6,800 saved</strong>.</p>

<h3>Indirect cost</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Reputation</strong>: “hacked” site → lost prospects.</li>
  <li><strong>Google</strong>: degraded Core Web Vitals → lower rankings (−70% traffic).</li>
  <li><strong>Crisis</strong>: comms, compensation → €2,000–€10,000.</li>
</ul>

<h2>My recommendation: static sites = 0 mandatory maintenance</h2>
<p><strong>Static</strong>: HTML/CSS/JS, no CMS or DB, <strong>no updates</strong>, <strong>free hosting</strong>, <strong>max speed</strong>, <strong>security</strong>.</p>
<p>3‑year TCO: €2,490 (build) + €10/year (domain). WordPress: build + hosting + licenses + maintenance → €4,000–€8,000.</p>
<p>Evolutions? <em>Evolution plan</em>: €20/month (1h/month, rollover). Optional. See <a href="/en/blog/frais-caches-site-internet">hidden costs</a> and <a href="/en/blog/cout-site-vitrine-2025">2025 costs</a>.</p>

<h2>Verdict: maintenance is insurance</h2>
<p>Choose not by <em>monthly price</em>, but by your <strong>risk tolerance</strong>. Non‑critical showcase → freelancer €50–€100/month. Critical site → agency with SLA €150–€300/month.</p>
<p>Tight budget? <strong>Static</strong>: €0 mandatory maintenance, free hosting, top speed/SEO. Savings: €700–€2,500/year. Over 3 years: €2,100–€7,500.</p>

<h2>FAQ</h2>
<h3>Maintenance cost over 1 year?</h3>
<p>Freelancer: €600–€1,200 (€50–€100/month) + licenses €50–€400/year + hosting €50–€120/year. Agency: €1,800–€6,000/year, licenses often included.</p>
<h3>DIY, freelancer or agency?</h3>
<p>DIY if you’re a dev. Freelancer for non‑critical showcases and tight budgets. Agency for critical sites (e‑commerce, SLA needed).</p>
<h3>Real outage cost?</h3>
<p>Minor: €1,000–€5,000. Major: €10,000–€50,000 (cleaning, rebuild, lost activity, comms).</p>
<h3>Static site = truly €0 maintenance?</h3>
<p>Yes: no CMS/plugins/DB → no mandatory updates. Only recurring cost: domain (€10–€20/year). Optional evolutions via the <em>Evolution plan</em> (€20/month).</p>
<h3>Why is agency more expensive?</h3>
<p>Continuity, <strong>SLA</strong>, level‑3 support, reporting. For critical sites, the extra cost is <strong>insurance</strong> against catastrophic outages.</p>
