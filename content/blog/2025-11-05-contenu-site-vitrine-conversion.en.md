# What content turns visitors into clients?

<p>Your site is live. Traffic arrives: 100, 200, maybe 500 visitors per month. <strong>How many become clients?</strong></p>
<p>Stats show up: 98% leave without action. No call. No form. No quote request. <strong>The issue isn’t design — it’s strategy.</strong> Your content pleases, but doesn’t persuade.</p>
<p>Average B2B site converts <strong>2–5%</strong>. Up to 95% leave. Improving this relies on <strong>web psychology</strong> and <strong>strategic content structure</strong>.</p>

<h2>Trust and readability first</h2>
<p>Visitors ask in 10 seconds: <em>What do you offer?</em> <em>Is it my solution?</em> <em>Can I trust you?</em> <em>What does it cost?</em> <em>How do I contact you?</em></p>
<p>Fail to answer → they leave.</p>

<h2>Long‑tail keywords drive intent</h2>
<p>Long tails (e.g. “custom kitchen carpenter Bordeaux”) convert <strong>2.5× more</strong> than generic terms. Each service page must target <strong>one intent</strong>.</p>

<h2>Pillar 1 — USP: why you, not someone else</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Weak: “Carpentry since 1985”</li>
  <li>Strong: “Solid‑wood custom kitchen in 3 days, delivered in 4 weeks. 10‑year warranty.”</li>
</ul>
<p>Back your USP with <strong>evidence</strong>: numbers, deadlines, testimonials.</p>

<h2>Pillar 2 — Transparent pricing</h2>
<p>Hide pricing → visitor leaves. Show <strong>ranges</strong> or <strong>tiered pricing</strong> (Essential / Professional / Premium). The middle tier becomes the “reasonable anchor”.</p>
<p>Optimize CTAs: “Request my free quote”, “See a similar project”. Measure click‑through and drop‑off. See <a href="/en/pricing-2025">Pricing 2025</a>.</p>

<h2>Pillar 3 — Social proof</h2>
<p>Add <strong>specific testimonials</strong> (details, name + city), <strong>short videos</strong>, <strong>case studies</strong> with numbers. Link your <strong>Google reviews</strong> (GMB).</p>
<p>Avoid generic praise. Prefer concrete, measurable outcomes.</p>

<h2>Pillar 4 — Visuals optimized for speed & SEO</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>WebP</strong> compression, 120–200 KB per image.</li>
  <li>Useful width ≈ 1200 px (no need for 4000 px).</li>
  <li>Descriptive filenames and rich <em>alt</em> with long‑tail terms.</li>
</ul>
<p>Goal: <strong>PageSpeed ≥ 90/100</strong>. A 3‑second site loses 50% of visitors.</p>

<h2>Site architecture: how many pages?</h2>
<p>Multi‑page beats one‑page almost always. <strong>Minimum 4–5 pages</strong> to cover the funnel.</p>

<p>Add if budget allows: Projects, Testimonials, FAQ, Blog.</p>

<h2>Content mistakes that kill conversion</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Talking about yourself instead of <strong>client problems/benefits</strong>.</li>
  <li>Hiding prices (show ranges/tiers).</li>
  <li>No social proof (testimonials, reviews, cases).</li>
  <li>Unoptimized images (slow site, low scores).</li>
  <li>Too many average pages; prefer 4–8 <strong>optimized</strong> pages.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Verdict: 4–5 optimized pages > 15 average pages</h2>
<p>To convert: <strong>clear USP</strong>, <strong>transparent pricing</strong>, <strong>social proof</strong>, <strong>optimized visuals</strong>. Structure 4–8 useful pages, target long‑tail intent, measure and iterate.</p>

<h2>FAQ</h2>
<h3>How many pages for a showcase site?</h3>
<p>Minimum 4: Home, Services (one per main service), About, Contact. Recommended: 5–8 with Projects, Testimonials, FAQ. Each service page targets a specific long‑tail intent.</p>
<h3>Should I show prices on my site?</h3>
<p>Yes, unless cases vary wildly (then “on quote”). Otherwise <strong>tiered pricing</strong> (Essential / Professional / Premium). Anchoring effect: the middle tier looks more attractive. Optimize CTA placement.</p>
<h3>How to present testimonials that convert?</h3>
<p>Specifics (details, deadlines, outcome), name + city, ideally <strong>video</strong>. Avoid anonymous or vague reviews. Integrate Google reviews and quantified case studies.</p>
<h3>How to optimize images?</h3>
<p><strong>WebP</strong>, width ≈ 1200 px, 120–200 KB, descriptive names, rich <em>alt</em>. Aim PageSpeed ≥ 90/100.</p>
<h3>What conversion rate should I aim for?</h3>
<p>B2B: <strong>2–5%</strong>. Below 1%: issue (message, CTAs, speed, proof). Improve the 4 pillars, test and measure.</p>
